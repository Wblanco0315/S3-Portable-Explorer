## Brand & Style (Dark Mode)

The design system shifts from high-energy neon aesthetics to a disciplined, "Pro Tool" environment. It is engineered for high-density information environments—technical dashboards, engineering interfaces, and data-heavy applications. The brand personality is clinical, reliable, and unobtrusive.

The design style is **Minimalist-Technical**. It utilizes a dark, low-saturation canvas with flat surfaces and precision-engineered strokes. By removing all glows and vibrant light-leaks, the focus is redirected toward content clarity and functional hierarchy. The aesthetic draws from high-end hardware interfaces and tactical military equipment, emphasizing utility over decoration.

## Colors (Dark Mode)

The palette is anchored in desaturated, matte tones that reduce eye strain during long-term use.

- **Primary (Slate Blue):** A muted, professional blue used for primary actions and focused states. It is purposeful and calm, lacking any fluorescent qualities.
- **Secondary (Storm Grey):** Used for inactive states, secondary buttons, and structural boundaries.
- **Tertiary (Frost Teal):** An accent color for data visualization or specific "success" indicators, providing just enough contrast without breaking the matte philosophy.
- **Neutral/Surface:** The background is a deep, desaturated charcoal (#1a1d23), with container layers shifting through subtle increments of grey to create hierarchy without the need for shadows.

## Brand & Style (Light Mode)
The design system focuses on a high-utility, professional aesthetic rooted in **Minimalism** and **Modern Corporate** principles. It prioritizes clarity, structural integrity, and cognitive ease. The target audience includes enterprise professionals, researchers, and technical operators who require a sober, distraction-free environment for complex tasks.

The visual language is "Matte": it avoids glossy finishes, dramatic gradients, or neon accents. Instead, it relies on a flat, precise execution where hierarchy is communicated through subtle tonal shifts and impeccable typography. The emotional response is one of reliability, precision, and calm efficiency.

## Colors (Light Mode)
This design system utilizes a refined light palette designed for maximum legibility. 
- **Primary (#4C6B91):** A weighted "Steel Blue" adjusted for higher contrast against light surfaces. Used for primary actions and active states.
- **Surface & Background:** Pure white (#FFFFFF) is the base for the canvas, while an off-white/light-slate (#F8FAFC) is used for secondary containers and sidebars to provide soft structural separation.
- **Neutral/Borders:** A cool-toned light gray (#D8DEE9) is used for dividers and ghost borders, maintaining the flat aesthetic without adding visual weight.
- **Typography:** Text uses a deep Slate/Navy scale (#2E3440) to ensure AAA accessibility and a professional, "ink-on-paper" feel.

## Typography
The typography system uses a tri-font strategy to reinforce the professional, technical nature of the interface. 

1. **Headlines:** **Hanken Grotesk** provides a sharp, contemporary geometric feel that looks authoritative in bold weights.
2. **Body:** **Inter** is used for all long-form reading and interface text, chosen for its exceptional legibility and neutral tone.
3. **Labels/Data:** **JetBrains Mono** is utilized for metadata, tags, and technical values to provide a "precision instrument" aesthetic.

Line heights are generous to ensure readability in data-dense layouts. Letter spacing is slightly tightened on large headings and opened up for small mono-spaced labels.

## Layout & Spacing
The layout follows a **Fixed-Fluid Hybrid Grid** based on a 4px baseline. 
- **Desktop:** 12-column grid with a max-width of 1440px. Gutters are fixed at 24px.
- **Tablet:** 8-column grid with 24px margins.
- **Mobile:** 4-column grid with 16px margins.

Spacing is used to create "grouping by proximity." Elements within a component use `sm` (8px) or `md` (16px) spacing, while major section breaks use `xl` (40px) or `xxl` (64px) to maintain a sense of openness and high-end whitespace.

## Elevation & Depth
In keeping with the "Matte" philosophy, this design system avoids traditional drop shadows. Depth is conveyed exclusively through **Tonal Layers** and **Low-Contrast Outlines**.

- **Level 0 (Background):** #FFFFFF.
- **Level 1 (Default Surface):** #F8FAFC. Used for cards and secondary sidebars.
- **Level 2 (Inset/Active):** #E5E9F0. Used for input fields, wells, or depressed states.

Borders are the primary tool for hierarchy. Use a 1px solid border (#D8DEE9) to define interactive elements. For "elevated" modals, instead of a shadow, use a thicker 2px border or a subtle dark-tinted ghost outline to maintain the flat, physical look.

## Shapes
The shape language is "Soft" yet disciplined. A base radius of **0.25rem (4px)** is applied to standard components (buttons, inputs) to take the edge off the brutalist origins while remaining professional. Larger containers like cards use **0.5rem (8px)**. 

Pills and circles are strictly reserved for status indicators and notification badges, never for primary structural elements, to maintain the architectural, grid-based aesthetic.

## Components
- **Buttons:** Primary buttons are solid Steel Blue (#4C6B91) with white text. Secondary buttons use a 1px border (#D8DEE9) with text in Slate (#2E3440). Interaction states are signaled by a 10% darkening of the fill—never a glow.
- **Inputs:** Use a #F8FAFC background with a #D8DEE9 bottom border or full ghost border. Focus states transition the border to #4C6B91 with a 1px weight increase.
- **Chips/Tags:** Small-scale components using JetBrains Mono. Use subtle background tints (e.g., light blue tint for primary tags) with high-contrast text.
- **Cards:** Flat containers with a 1px border. No shadows. Use a header area with a slight #F8FAFC fill to separate titles from content.
- **Lists:** Clean rows separated by 1px dividers (#E5E9F0). Hover states use a subtle #F8FAFC background shift.
- **Data Tables:** High-density, utilizing JetBrains Mono for numeric values. Sticky headers use a #F8FAFC background to maintain context during scroll.
